</a>Hope you've all settled back into the new year nicely! This month's meeting is on Monday 16th February</strong>, 7pm for 7.15pm start, at The Skiff</a>, and we're excited to be partaking in the "Wisdom as a Service</a>" world tour! We're welcoming Yitzchok Willroth</strong>, or coderabbi</a> as he may be more familiarly known on Twitter, to present to the group:</p>

All aboard as we travel from London to Chicago! These two historical methods approach unit testing from divergent perspectives. During our voyage, we'll explore each style's philosophy; not only how they test, but how they view testing, what they test and why. We'll discuss the merits of the approaches and how to implement each style. Finally, we'll develop a conceptual model for integrating other resources encountered along your testing journey.</blockquote>

Yitzchok Willroth is an independent application developer and development consultant living in Lakewood, NJ. A start-up vet, dev bootcamp instructor and frequent conference speaker, Yitzchok is the organizer of the ShorePHP User Group and an active participant in the PHPMentoring Initiative. Yitzchok is also the driving force behind the Wisdom as a Service World Tour</a>, a year long effort to engage over 50 User Groups around the world in a conversation about Software Craftsmanship, Personal Growth and Professional Development. Passionate about clean code and robust application architecture, he is also a strong proponent of remote teams as a way for organizations to thrive while affording developers a healthy work:life balance.</em></p>

Yitz is a keynote speaker at this year's PHPUK conference</a> in London the week of the meeting, and this promises to be an excellent talk!</p>

As usual the BrightonPHP event is listed over on joind.in</a> and Lanyrd</a> - please sign up if you're planning to attend.</p>
